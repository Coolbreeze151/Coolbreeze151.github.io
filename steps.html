NUS Computing STePS Term Project 2017/18

If you are looking to learn all about front end development for enterprise systems, then the NUS School of Computing's IS3106 is the module for you to take! It was a gruelling 3 months but I am proud to say that I have learnt a lot at the end of it all. Having had to develop a prototype project for our term showcase, we managed to clinch the 3rd prize out of a total of 15 teams! Our group was interested in the F&B industry & conceptualized Makan Maker, a platform that would deliver customers prepared ingredients & a recipe for them to cook their own meals. As part of our project, we developed a web & mobile application. For our web application, we had to use new technologies learnt from class to develop it. These technologies were mainly JavaServer Faces (JSF) & Primefaces. Java EE was the main platform we had to use to develop our application. We also adopted Dynamic HTML Rendering which allowed us to generate multiple web pages by using information from the database. For example, having a simple script implemented meant that there was little need to manually create multiple web pages for each catalogue product thus reducing the development time & effort.

Additionally, our group created a mobile application that could integrate with our web platform. In terms of stack, we used the open source ionic framework to develop & integrate our mobile application with our web platform. Besides Java, we also had to pick up AngularJS & Typescript. In terms of integration, we also had to learn how to use Restful API to ensure that our mobile application was able to interact with the same database adopted by our web platform.

Completing this project was an amazing experience. Having had to learn many new stacks within a short period of time in contrast to an average person who on average would take a year to master a new development stack was a great accomplishment. Clinching 3rd prize for the term showcase also made our accomplishments much sweeter as a team. The module IS3106 is definitely one I would recommend to others looking for a challenge & personal growth!
